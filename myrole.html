Core Leadership Pillars

The principles that guide my academic journey and technical projects.

🎯

Systemic Responsibility

I view responsibility as full ownership. Whether leading an FLL robotics team or an IGCSE group project, I take the lead in organizing timelines, identifying logic-gate bottlenecks in our designs, and ensuring every task is completed to an aerospace-grade standard.

🤝

Contextual Empathy

Effective leadership requires understanding the team. I prioritize observing the unique strengths of my classmates, ensuring everyone is supported during high-pressure exam weeks and that our collaborative efforts are inclusive and efficient.

🌍

Service & Mentorship

Service is about consistency. I go beyond individual success by creating revision resources and simplified technical guides for complex Chemistry and English topics, aiming to elevate the collective academic standard of Year 10.

⚖️

Radical Integrity

Integrity is the baseline of engineering. I maintain honesty in my code—as seen in projects like Architect OS—and in my academic research, building a foundation of trust that is essential for long-term professional respect.

2026 Strategic Plan

My roadmap for creating a visible, lasting impact within the school community.

01 // Technical Innovation & Peer Growth

My primary goal is to foster a culture of creative thinking. By promoting student-led ideas—particularly in software development and robotics—I aim to show that age is not a barrier to innovation. I will work to ensure peers feel confident in proposing concepts that solve real school challenges.

As a student following the rigorous IGCSE curriculum, I recognize that success shouldn't lead to burnout. I plan to introduce initiatives that focus on balanced study habits and stress reduction, ensuring our community remains both high-achieving and healthy.
